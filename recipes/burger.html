Classic Juicy Beef Burger

Description

Nothing beats a classic, restaurant-quality hamburger made right at home. The secret to keeping the patties completely juicy and flavorful is using ground beef with an 80/20 lean-to-fat ratio and not over-handling the meat while shaping it.

Topped with melted cheddar cheese, fresh lettuce, and tomatoes on a golden-brown toasted brioche bun.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs ground chuck beef (80% lean / 20% fat mix)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • 6 Burger buns (Brioche preferred!)
  • 6 slices of cheddar or American cheese
  • Fresh toppings: lettuce leaves, tomato slices, red onion, and pickles

Steps

  1. Place your ground beef in a large bowl and gently loosen it up with your hands.
  2. Sprinkle the garlic powder, salt, pepper, and Worcestershire sauce evenly over the meat.
  3. Mix the meat gently with your hands just until combined—do not over-mix or the burger will become tough!
  4. Divide the beef into 6 portions and shape them into patties about 1-inch wider than your buns (they shrink when cooking).
  5. Press a small dent into the center of each patty using your thumb so they stay flat while grilling.
  6. Cook on a hot skillet or grill for 3-5 minutes per side. Add the cheese in the last minute of cooking so it melts perfectly.
  7. Toast your burger buns, stack your favorite veggies on the bottom bun, place the cheesy patty on top, and enjoy!
